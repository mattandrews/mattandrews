I was tucking into a greasy burger a few weeks ago in Covent Garden's trendy MEATmarket when a crowd of hip young things wandered in. "That guy's wearing Google Glass," my girlfriend observed, nodding at one of the party. "No way," I said, craning my neck to see while trying to remain nonchalant. It was true. The man by the bar had a pair of neat, curious glasses on, and was remarkably casual about the fact. We finished our food speculating about the gadget and how he'd managed to blag a pair. Upon leaving I couldn't help myself and had to ask the wearer.

"Are they … ?"
